Visually Inspect Normalcy

Interpreting a new type of plot: the Q$\sim$Q plot.

We plot the data (Y axis) against the corresponding quantiles of a theoretical normal distribution (X-axis) and then plot a line through the $25^{th}\%$ and $75_{th}\%$ quantiles.
If our data are normally distributed, all the quantiles will match the line.

Testing for Normalcy

There is a number of statistical test that can give you a p-value on the normal-nessof your data.

One of which is called the Shapiro-Wilks Test:

$$W={\left(\sum _{i=1}^{n}a_{i}x_{(i)}\right)^{2} \over \sum _{i=1}^{n}(x_{i}-{\overline {x}})^{2}}$$

Based on both visual inspection, QQ-plot, and the Shapiro Wilks test, we conclude that nither data are normally distributed.

We can also use a test of Equal Variance, namely the Fligner-Killeen test.
